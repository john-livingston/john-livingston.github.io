What I Do

I'm an astronomer at the University of Tokyo researching exoplanets, starting with the discovery of their transits using data from space-based missions like Kepler, K2, and TESS. By conducting transit follow-up observations using the Spitzer Space Telescope and high-precision ground-based instruments, I refine estimates of their radii and orbital periods, ensuring the feasibility of their study with future missions like JWST. Using stabilized high-precision spectrographs, I measure their orbits and masses to constrain their mean densities and bulk compositions.
